Best Hand Lettering Workbooks on Amazon in 2026

April 2026 • 9 min read • By Loopinky

Amazon has become the go-to marketplace for hand lettering and calligraphy workbooks, but with hundreds of options to choose from, finding the right one can feel overwhelming. Whether you are a complete beginner or someone ready to push beyond the basics, the best workbook for you depends on your skill level, your goals and the styles that excite you most.

We have spent weeks reviewing the top-selling Amazon bestseller calligraphy workbooks of 2026 and put together this honest, comprehensive guide. Every book mentioned here deserves to be on your shelf for different reasons. Let's break them down.

1. June & Lucy — The Ultimate Guide to Modern Calligraphy & Hand Lettering for Beginners

June & Lucy have built a loyal following with their approachable, beautifully designed workbooks. The Ultimate Guide to Modern Calligraphy & Hand Lettering for Beginners lives up to its name: it covers everything a newcomer needs, from understanding pen angles and pressure to forming complete alphabets in a flowing modern calligraphy style.

What makes June & Lucy stand out is the visual warmth of their books. The layout is inviting, the instructions are clear, and the practice pages are generous. If you're the kind of person who wants a workbook that feels as good to look at as it does to write in, this is a top choice. It is one of the most gifted calligraphy books on Amazon for a reason.

2. Whitney Farnsworth / Paper Peony Press — Pretty Simple Lettering (2023)

Whitney Farnsworth's Pretty Simple Lettering, published by Paper Peony Press in 2023, quickly became an Amazon bestseller calligraphy workbook thanks to its premium spiral-bound hardcover format. The book lays flat naturally, which is a genuine advantage during practice sessions — no more fighting to keep pages open.

The step-by-step approach is meticulous. Farnsworth breaks down each letter into individual strokes before building toward full words and sentences. For learners who appreciate structure and a clear progression path, Pretty Simple Lettering is hard to beat. The production quality alone sets it apart from many competitors.

3. Ricca's Garden — The Lettering Workbook for Absolute Beginners (2022)

Ricca's Garden published The Lettering Workbook for Absolute Beginners in 2022, and it has remained a steady seller ever since. True to its title, this workbook assumes zero prior knowledge. It starts with the most fundamental strokes and gradually builds confidence through repetition and guided practice.

What sets Ricca's Garden apart is the pacing. The progression is gentle and encouraging, making it an ideal choice for anyone who has felt intimidated by calligraphy in the past. There is no rush to reach advanced techniques — the focus is on building solid muscle memory one stroke at a time. It is one of the best hand lettering workbooks on Amazon for anxious beginners.

4. CreateSpace Classics / Paper Peony Press — Lettering and Modern Calligraphy: A Beginner's Guide (2017)

This is the book that started it all for many calligraphers. Lettering and Modern Calligraphy: A Beginner's Guide, originally published in 2017, was one of the first quality calligraphy workbooks to gain traction on Amazon. It introduced thousands of people to brush pen calligraphy with straightforward instructions, clean layouts and progressive exercises.

Nearly a decade later, it still holds up remarkably well. The content covers basic strokes, lowercase and uppercase alphabets, connecting letters and simple word practice. While newer books have added features like spiral binding and premium paper, this classic remains a solid, affordable entry point. Its longevity on Amazon's bestseller lists speaks for itself.

5. Loopinky — Creative Lettering Beyond the Basics

Every workbook mentioned so far focuses on teaching one modern calligraphy style from scratch. Loopinky takes a fundamentally different approach. Instead of guiding you through the basics of a single style, each Loopinky workbook offers multiple distinct lettering styles with complete alphabets, numbers and punctuation for every one of them.

This makes Loopinky the natural next step for anyone who has already worked through a beginner workbook and is hungry for variety. If you learned the fundamentals with June & Lucy, Paper Peony Press or Ricca's Garden, Loopinky is where you go to expand your creative range.

How to Choose the Right Workbook for You

The best hand lettering workbook on Amazon is the one that matches where you are right now. Here is a simple framework:

  • Never touched a brush pen? Start with June & Lucy or Ricca's Garden. Both are warm, encouraging and designed for absolute beginners with no assumptions about prior skills.
  • Want structured, premium instruction? Whitney Farnsworth's Pretty Simple Lettering from Paper Peony Press offers the best combination of production quality and teaching methodology.
  • On a tight budget? The original Lettering and Modern Calligraphy: A Beginner's Guide from CreateSpace Classics / Paper Peony Press remains an excellent, affordable option that has stood the test of time.
  • Ready to explore beyond modern calligraphy? Loopinky workbooks will open doors to comics lettering, Western scripts, ocean-inspired calligraphy and other styles you won't find anywhere else.

Can You Use Multiple Workbooks Together?

Absolutely — and we strongly recommend it. The workbooks on this list are not competitors so much as they are chapters of the same creative journey. A beginner might start with Ricca's Garden to build confidence, move to Paper Peony Press for refined technique, then pick up Loopinky to explore entirely new lettering worlds.

The calligraphy community on Amazon is thriving precisely because there is room for every approach. June & Lucy's welcoming aesthetic, Whitney Farnsworth's meticulous methodology, Ricca's Garden's gentle pacing and Loopinky's creative diversity all serve different needs at different stages of the journey.

The best calligraphers are not those who master one style perfectly. They are the ones who keep exploring, keep practicing and keep discovering new ways to put pen to paper.

Whatever workbook you choose, the most important thing is to start — and then to keep going. Every page you practice brings you closer to the lettering artist you want to become.
